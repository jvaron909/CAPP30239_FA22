Chicago's structure of governance has been referred to as a strong mayor-weak council system. The City Council reports to the Mayor as she serves as its president and has significant powers such as appointing committee chairs, filling vacancies, and determining its operating budget, among others. As a result of this imbalance of power, the Council's capacity to actively debate the legislation presented is limited
